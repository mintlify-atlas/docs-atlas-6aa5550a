## Progress Overview

Sociales provides several ways to track your learning journey as you work through grade levels, lessons, and quizzes. Understanding these indicators helps you monitor your improvement and identify areas that need more practice.

## Visual Progress Indicators

### Grade Level Navigation
Your current learning path is visible through the navigation system:

- **Home Page**: Shows all available grade levels
- **Lessons List**: Displays the grade you're currently exploring
- **Lesson View**: Shows which specific lesson you're studying

<Info>
  Navigation breadcrumbs help you understand your location:
  - "← Volver a Materias" (return to subjects)
  - "← Volver a Grados" (return to grades)
  - "← Volver a Lecciones" (return to lessons)
</Info>

## Quiz Performance Tracking

### Real-Time Feedback
As you take quizzes, the system provides immediate feedback:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Answer Indication">
    Each answer you select triggers instant visual feedback:
    - **Correct answers**: Green highlight with "¡Correcto! 🎉"
    - **Incorrect answers**: Red highlight with "Incorrecto 😔"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Score Accumulation">
    Your score increases in real-time:
    - Starts at 0 points
    - Increases by 1 for each correct answer
    - Stays the same for incorrect answers
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Progress Counter">
    Track which question you're on:
    - Displayed as "Pregunta X de 5" at the top of each question
    - Shows both current position and total questions
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Final Results Display

After completing a quiz, the results screen shows:

- **Your Score**: Large number showing points earned (0-5)
- **Total Possible**: "DE 5 PUNTOS" indicating the maximum score
- **Completion Status**: "¡Increíble Trabajo!" message confirming completion

<Tip>
  The results screen always displays your score prominently, making it easy to track improvement across multiple attempts.
</Tip>

## Understanding Your Performance

### Score Interpretation

| Score | Performance Level | Next Steps |
|-------|------------------|------------|
| 5/5 | Excellent | Move to next lesson with confidence |
| 4/5 | Very Good | Strong understanding, minor review may help |
| 3/5 | Good | Review the lesson and try again |
| 2/5 | Needs Improvement | Re-read lesson content before retrying |
| 0-1/5 | Review Required | Study the lesson material thoroughly |

<Note>
  Because questions are randomized from a larger pool, each quiz attempt may have different questions. This means you can practice multiple times to reinforce learning.
</Note>

## Tracking Lesson Completion

### Linear Progression
While Sociales doesn't enforce a strict order, a recommended learning path is:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Your Grade Level">
    Start by choosing the grade that matches your current education level or interests.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Browse Available Lessons">
    Review all lessons in the grade to understand what topics are covered.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete Lessons Sequentially">
    Work through lessons in order, as they may build on previous concepts.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Take Each Quiz">
    Complete the quiz at the end of each lesson before moving to the next one.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Retry Low Scores">
    If you score 3 or below, consider reviewing the lesson and retrying the quiz.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Self-Monitoring Strategies

### Keep Track of Your Journey

Since the platform focuses on learning rather than formal progress tracking, you can monitor your own progress:

**Mental Checklist**
- Note which lessons you've completed
- Remember your quiz scores for each lesson
- Identify topics where you scored lower and need review

**Learning Indicators**
- **First Attempt Scores**: Gauge initial understanding
- **Retry Scores**: Measure improvement after review
- **Time to Complete**: Notice if lessons become easier over time

<Tip>
  Consider keeping a notebook to track:
  - Lessons completed
  - Quiz scores (1st attempt vs retries)
  - Topics that need more study
  - Questions you found challenging
</Tip>

## Improving Your Performance

### Strategies for Better Results

<Steps>
  <Step title="Study Before Quizzing">
    Read all lesson content thoroughly before attempting the quiz. Don't skip sections or videos.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Incorrect Answers">
    When you answer incorrectly, the system shows the correct answer. Take note of these for future reference.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Use the Retry Feature">
    Click **Intentar de Nuevo** to practice with new random questions from the same lesson.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Revisit Lesson Content">
    Use the back link to return to the lesson and review specific sections before retrying the quiz.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Practice Across Grades">
    If one grade level is too challenging, try a different grade to build foundational knowledge.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Navigation as Progress Tracking

### Using Breadcrumbs
The navigation system helps you understand where you are in your learning journey:

**Three-Level Hierarchy**
1. **Subjects** (Materias) - Top level
2. **Grades** (Grados) - Grade selection (4to, 5to, 6to, Resumen)
3. **Lessons** (Lecciones) - Individual lesson topics
4. **Quiz** - Assessment within each lesson

<Info>
  You can jump back to any level using the navigation links:
  - From quiz/lesson content → Lessons list
  - From lessons list → Grade selection  
  - From grade selection → Subject selection
</Info>

## Setting Personal Goals

### Recommended Milestones

**Short-term Goals**
- Complete one lesson per study session
- Score at least 4/5 on your first quiz attempt
- Review any lesson where you scored below 3/5

**Medium-term Goals**
- Finish all lessons in one grade level
- Retry quizzes until you achieve 5/5 on each
- Complete all three primary grades (4to, 5to, 6to)

**Long-term Goals**
- Master all grade-level content
- Complete the Resumen General for MEP 2026 preparation
- Consistently score 5/5 on quiz retries

<Note>
  The Resumen General is designed for comprehensive exam preparation and contains material spanning multiple grade levels. Save this for last or use it for intensive review.
</Note>

## Learning at Your Own Pace

Sociales is designed for self-paced learning:

- **No time limits**: Take as long as you need on each lesson
- **Unlimited retries**: Practice quizzes as many times as you want
- **Flexible navigation**: Jump between grades and lessons freely
- **Independent study**: Learn without pressure or external deadlines

<Tip>
  The most effective learning happens when you:
  1. Study content thoroughly
  2. Test yourself with the quiz
  3. Review mistakes
  4. Retry until you achieve mastery
  5. Move to the next lesson only when confident
</Tip>
